# Step 2: Voice & Tone

**Progress: Step 2 of 3** — Next: Visual Identity

## STEP GOAL

Define how the brand speaks — its voice attributes, tone variations across contexts, and concrete do/don't examples.

## MANDATORY RULES

- Voice is consistent; tone adapts to context
- Every guideline needs an example — abstract rules don't work
- Include "we don't" examples — knowing what to avoid is as important as knowing what to do

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

1. If messaging framework exists, pull voice attributes from it. Otherwise:
   - "Describe your brand in 3-4 adjectives"
   - "What brands do you admire for how they communicate?"
   - "What communication style would turn off your audience?"
2. Define **Voice Attributes** (3-4):

   | Attribute | We Are | We Are Not | Example |
   |-----------|--------|-----------|---------|
   | {{e.g., Bold}} | {{Confident, direct}} | {{Aggressive, arrogant}} | {{example copy}} |

3. Define **Tone by Context**:
   - Website / Landing Pages — {{tone + example}}
   - Social Media — {{tone + example}}
   - Email Marketing — {{tone + example}}
   - Customer Support — {{tone + example}}
   - Sales / Proposals — {{tone + example}}
   - Error Messages / Bad News — {{tone + example}}
4. Create **Writing Do's and Don'ts**:
   - Vocabulary preferences (use/don't use)
   - Sentence style (short/long, active/passive)
   - Jargon policy
   - Humor policy
   - Emoji / punctuation policy
5. Write **Sample Copy** showing the voice in action:
   - A short social post
   - An email subject line
   - A homepage headline
   - A CTA button
6. Present and ask: "Does this sound like your brand?"
