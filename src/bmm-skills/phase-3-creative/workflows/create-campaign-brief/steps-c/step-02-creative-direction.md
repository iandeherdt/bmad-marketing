# Step 2: Creative Direction

**Progress: Step 2 of 3** — Next: Deliverable Specs

## STEP GOAL

Define the creative direction — tone, visual style, headline concepts, and the emotional territory the campaign should own.

## MANDATORY RULES

- Creative direction must serve the objective, not just look cool
- Provide specific direction, not vague adjectives ("modern and clean" means nothing)
- Include examples or references when possible
- The creative concept should be campaign-able — it should work across multiple assets

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

1. Propose a creative concept:
   - **Campaign Concept** — The big idea in one sentence
   - **Why It Works** — How it connects to audience pain points and the key message
2. Define the tone and mood:
   - "How should this campaign make people feel?"
   - Propose 3-4 tone words (e.g., "bold, urgent, empowering, real")
   - Show what the tone is NOT (e.g., "Not corporate, not timid, not salesy")
3. Headline concepts — Propose 3-5 headline directions:
   - Each with a rationale for why it works
   - Different angles/approaches to test
4. Visual direction (describe in text):
   - **Color Palette** — Mood and energy
   - **Imagery Style** — Photography vs illustration, lifestyle vs abstract
   - **Typography Feel** — Bold vs elegant, modern vs classic
   - **Layout Approach** — Clean vs busy, hero image vs text-forward
5. Reference examples:
   - "Think [reference campaign/brand] meets [reference campaign/brand]"
   - Describe 2-3 reference campaigns or styles for inspiration
6. Present creative direction and ask: "Does this feel right? Too safe? Too bold?"
