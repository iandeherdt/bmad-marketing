# Step 4: Campaign Plan

**Progress: Step 4 of 4** — Final Step

## STEP GOAL

Create a concrete campaign plan with themes, timeline, KPIs, and resource requirements that brings the strategy to life.

## MANDATORY RULES

- Campaigns must tie back to positioning and messaging from Step 2
- Include specific KPIs with targets, not vague goals
- Timeline must be realistic for available resources
- Every campaign needs a clear objective and success criteria

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

1. Ask: "What's your planning horizon? (30 days, 90 days, 6 months, 12 months)"
2. Ask: "What are the top 2-3 marketing objectives for this period?"
3. For each objective, build a campaign:
   - **Campaign Name** — Working title
   - **Objective** — What specific outcome?
   - **Target Audience** — Which persona(s)?
   - **Key Message** — From the messaging framework
   - **Channels** — Which channels from Step 3?
   - **Tactics** — Specific actions (content pieces, ads, emails, etc.)
   - **Timeline** — Start date, milestones, end date
   - **Budget** — Estimated cost
   - **KPIs & Targets** — Specific metrics with numbers
   - **Owner** — Who's responsible?
4. Create an overall marketing calendar showing campaigns over time
5. Define **global KPIs** that roll up across campaigns:
   - Awareness metrics (reach, impressions, brand search volume)
   - Consideration metrics (website traffic, content engagement, email subscribers)
   - Conversion metrics (leads, MQLs, SQLs, customers)
   - Efficiency metrics (CAC, CPA, ROI)
6. Present the complete campaign plan
7. Save to `docs/output/marketing-strategy.md`
