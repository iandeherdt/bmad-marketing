# Step 3: Channel Strategy

**Progress: Step 3 of 4** — Next: Campaign Plan

## STEP GOAL

Select marketing channels, allocate resources, and define the role each channel plays in the marketing mix.

## MANDATORY RULES

- Channel selection must align with where the audience actually is (from research)
- Be realistic about resources — don't recommend 10 channels for a 2-person team
- Every channel needs a clear role (awareness, consideration, conversion, retention)

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

1. Present channel options based on audience and budget:
   - **Organic**: SEO/Content, Social (LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Instagram, TikTok, etc.), Community, Partnerships, PR
   - **Paid**: Search ads, Social ads, Display/Retargeting, Sponsorships, Influencers
   - **Owned**: Email/Newsletter, Blog, Webinars/Events, Product-led
2. For each relevant channel, ask:
   - "Are you active here? What's worked?"
   - "Does your audience spend time here?"
3. Recommend a channel mix with:
   - **Primary channels** (2-3) — Heavy investment, core focus
   - **Secondary channels** (1-2) — Supporting role, lighter investment
   - **Experimental** (0-1) — Test with small budget
4. For each selected channel, define:
   - **Role** — What funnel stage does it serve?
   - **Content Type** — What content format works here?
   - **Cadence** — How often to post/publish?
   - **Budget Split** — Percentage of resources
   - **KPIs** — How to measure success?
5. Present the channel strategy and ask: "Does this feel right given your resources?"
