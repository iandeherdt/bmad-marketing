# Step 1: Situation Analysis

**Progress: Step 1 of 4** — Next: Positioning & Messaging

## STEP GOAL

Assess the current marketing situation — where you stand, what's working, and what needs to change.

## MANDATORY RULES

- If a market research brief exists, use it as the starting point
- Be honest about weaknesses — a strategy built on denial fails
- Quantify wherever possible (even rough estimates)

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

1. If research brief exists, summarize key findings. If not, gather basics:
   - "What marketing are you doing today?"
   - "What's working? What's not?"
   - "What's your current awareness/pipeline/conversion like?"
2. Build a SWOT analysis:
   - **Strengths** — What marketing advantages do you have?
   - **Weaknesses** — Where are the gaps?
   - **Opportunities** — What market opportunities can marketing exploit?
   - **Threats** — What competitive or market threats should we plan for?
3. Assess current state:
   - **Brand Awareness** — How well known are you? (low/growing/established)
   - **Lead Generation** — What's your current pipeline?
   - **Content** — What content exists? Quality?
   - **Channels** — Where are you active?
   - **Team & Budget** — What resources are available?
4. Present the situation analysis and ask: "Does this accurately capture where we are?"
