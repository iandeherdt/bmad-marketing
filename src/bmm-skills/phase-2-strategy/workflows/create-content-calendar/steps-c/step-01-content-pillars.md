# Step 1: Content Pillars

**Progress: Step 1 of 3** — Next: Channel Mapping

## STEP GOAL

Define 3-5 content pillars — the thematic categories that all content will map to. Pillars ensure consistency and coverage across the marketing mix.

## MANDATORY RULES

- Pillars must connect to positioning and audience needs
- 3-5 pillars maximum — fewer is better for focus
- Each pillar needs a clear audience benefit, not just a topic

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

1. If marketing strategy/messaging framework exists, propose pillars based on value propositions and audience pain points
2. If not, ask:
   - "What topics does your audience care most about?"
   - "What expertise or perspective is unique to you?"
   - "What questions do your customers ask most?"
3. For each pillar, define:
   - **Pillar Name** — Short, memorable label
   - **Description** — What this pillar covers
   - **Audience Benefit** — Why the audience cares
   - **Example Topics** — 3-5 specific content ideas
   - **Content Split** — Rough percentage of total content
4. Present pillars and ask: "Do these capture what your content should be about?"

## MENU

- **[P] Proceed** — Pillars confirmed, move to Channel Mapping

## SUCCESS METRICS

- 3-5 pillars defined with clear audience benefit
- Content split totals 100%
- User confirms pillars feel right
