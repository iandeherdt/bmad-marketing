# Step 1: Define Research Scope

**Progress: Step 1 of 4** — Next: Audience Analysis

## STEP GOAL

Narrow down the research questions, define market boundaries, and identify the key unknowns that this research needs to address.

## MANDATORY RULES

- Do NOT skip this step — unfocused research produces unfocused results
- Ask the user questions; do not assume market boundaries
- If project context exists, reference it but still validate with the user

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

1. Ask the user: "What are the 2-3 most important questions you need this research to answer?"
2. Ask: "How do you define your market? What's in scope and what's out of scope?"
3. Ask: "What do you already know vs. what's genuinely unknown?"
4. Based on their answers, propose:
   - **Research Questions** (3-5 specific, answerable questions)
   - **Market Boundaries** (geography, industry, segment, price tier)
   - **Key Unknowns** (what we need to discover)
   - **Data Sources** (what we'll use to answer the questions)
5. Present the scope summary and ask for confirmation
