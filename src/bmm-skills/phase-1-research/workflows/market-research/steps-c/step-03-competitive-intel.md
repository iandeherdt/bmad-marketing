# Step 3: Competitive Intelligence

**Progress: Step 3 of 4** — Next: Synthesis

## STEP GOAL

Map the competitive landscape, analyze competitor positioning and tactics, and identify whitespace opportunities.

## MANDATORY RULES

- Analyze at least 3 competitors (or all if fewer than 3)
- Use the competitive landscape template for structured output
- Focus on marketing positioning and tactics, not just product features
- Identify gaps and opportunities, not just descriptions

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

1. Ask the user: "Who are your top 3-5 competitors? Include both direct and indirect competitors."
2. For each competitor, analyze:
   - **Positioning** — How do they position themselves? What's their core message?
   - **Target Audience** — Who are they going after? Same as you or different?
   - **Channels** — Where are they marketing? (social, content, paid, events, etc.)
   - **Content Strategy** — What content are they producing? What topics? What formats?
   - **Strengths** — What are they doing well in marketing?
   - **Weaknesses** — Where are they falling short or vulnerable?
   - **Pricing/Positioning Tier** — Premium, mid-market, budget?
3. Build a **Competitive Matrix** comparing all competitors across key dimensions
4. Identify:
   - **Market gaps** — Positioning or channels nobody owns
   - **Messaging opportunities** — Claims nobody is making
   - **Audience segments** — Underserved groups
   - **Content whitespace** — Topics or formats nobody covers well
5. Present findings and ask for user input on any competitors they know more about
