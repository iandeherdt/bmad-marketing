# Step 4: Recommendations

**Progress: Step 4 of 4** — Final Step

## STEP GOAL

Translate insights into specific, actionable recommendations — what to do more of, what to change, what to stop, and what to test.

## MANDATORY RULES

- Every recommendation must tie back to a specific insight
- Include effort/impact assessment for prioritization
- Recommend specific tests, not vague "optimize more"
- Be honest about what to stop — killing underperformers frees resources

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

1. Categorize recommendations:

   **Double Down** (high impact, already working):
   - What's working that deserves more investment?
   - Specific: increase budget by X%, expand to Y channel, etc.

   **Optimize** (promising but needs improvement):
   - What's showing potential but underperforming?
   - Specific changes: new creative, different targeting, adjusted timing

   **Test** (unknowns worth exploring):
   - What hypotheses emerged from the data?
   - Design specific A/B tests with hypothesis, variants, and success criteria
   - Use `templates/ab-test-report.md` format for test planning

   **Stop or Reduce** (low impact, draining resources):
   - What's not working and unlikely to improve?
   - Specific: pause X campaign, reduce Y channel spend, sunset Z content

2. **Prioritization Matrix:**

   | Recommendation | Impact | Effort | Priority |

   | {{}} | High/Med/Low | High/Med/Low | {{1-N}} |

3. **Budget Reallocation** (if applicable):
   - Current allocation vs. recommended allocation
   - Specific dollar/percentage shifts

4. **Next Period Goals:**
   - Updated KPI targets based on learnings
   - 3-5 specific goals for the next period

5. Save to `docs/output/performance-report-{{period}}.md` using `templates/performance-dashboard.md`

6. Present: "Here's my recommendation: [top 1-2 actions]. The biggest lever is [X]."
