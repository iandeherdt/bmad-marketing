## Brand Foundation

### Brand Story
R.T.A. Construct is a family construction company that believes building should be straightforward — fixed price, fixed team, no surprises. They exist because too many contractors overpromise and underdeliver, and they wanted to prove it doesn't have to be that way. Based in Tessenderlo-Ham, Limburg, they've built their reputation on doing exactly what they say they'll do — and now they're bringing that same reliability to property developers and investors across Limburg, Antwerp, and Flemish Brabant.

### Brand Values
1. **Transparency** — Fixed prices, clear agreements, no hidden costs. The budget you see is the budget you pay.
2. **Reliability** — Same team, same quality, every project. No subcontractor roulette.
3. **Accountability** — One contact person who owns the outcome. Your project manager is on-site, not behind a desk.
4. **Craftsmanship** — Pride in the work. Projects not handed over until standards are met.
5. **Partnership** — Long-term relationships, not transactional projects. We grow with our partners.

### Brand Personality
The experienced builder who's seen it all and has nothing to prove — confident without being flashy, direct without being cold, personal without being unprofessional. The person you trust with a big decision because they tell you what to expect and then deliver exactly that.

### Brand Archetype
**Caregiver-Builder hybrid** — Protects clients from risk (Caregiver) while creating something tangible and lasting (Creator). In B2B terms: the partner who has your back.

## Voice & Tone

### Voice Attributes

| Attribute | We Are | We Are Not | Example |
|-----------|--------|-----------|---------|
| **Direct** | Clear, factual, to the point. We say what we mean and back it with evidence. | Vague, salesy, full of filler words or marketing fluff. | "Vaste prijs. Vast team. Vaste planning." — not "We strive to deliver excellence at competitive rates." |
| **Confident** | We know what we're doing and we let the work prove it. Calm authority. | Arrogant, boastful, or making claims we can't back up. | "Opgeleverd. Op tijd. Op budget. Bekijk het project." — not "We're the best construction company in the region!" |
| **Warm** | Personal, human, first-name basis. The family company you actually want to work with. | Cold, corporate, impersonal. No "Dear Sir/Madam" energy. | "Vragen? Bel Ruben. Hij kent uw project." — not "Please contact our customer service department." |
| **Expert** | We share knowledge generously — regulations, processes, market insights. We earn trust by being useful. | Preachy, condescending, gatekeeping information. | "Nieuwe EPC-regels in 2026: dit verandert er voor uw project." — not "As industry-leading experts, we've been navigating complex regulations for decades." |

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|

| Difficult news | Honest, solutions-first, accountable | "We hebben een vertraging op materiaallevering. Hier is wat we doen om de impact te beperken, en de aangepaste planning." |

### Writing Guidelines

**Do:**
- Lead with the fact or the benefit, then explain
- Use short sentences. One idea per sentence.
- Use active voice: "Wij leveren op"
- Show numbers and specifics: "4,7/5 klantscore"
- Write in Dutch (Flemish) for local market. Keep it natural, not formal.
- Use "u" (formal you) for B2B, "je" for consumer content
- Name real people: "Uw projectleider Ruben"
- Use industry terms your audience knows (EPC, BTW, ruwbouw) for B2B
- Keep CTAs action-specific: "Bekijk het project", "Plan een kennismaking"

**Don't:**
- Start with "Wij zijn..." or company history
- Write long, nested sentences with multiple clauses
- Use passive voice: "Het project werd opgeleverd"
- Use vague superlatives: "uitstekende kwaliteit"
- Use overly formal or Netherlandic Dutch that feels distant
- Mix up formality within the same piece
- Hide behind anonymous roles: "Uw contactpersoon"
- Over-explain basics to professional audiences
- Use generic CTAs: "Klik hier", "Meer info"

|-----|-----------|

### Jargon Policy
Use construction terminology freely with B2B audiences (developers, investors, architects). For consumer content, explain or simplify.

### Humor Policy
Light, dry humor is welcome — especially on LinkedIn. Never sarcastic, never at anyone's expense. R.T.A.'s humor is the builder who smiles because the job went exactly as planned.

### Emoji Policy
Sparingly on LinkedIn (1-2 per post max, functional: 👇🏗️📍). Never on the website or in proposals. Never in email subject lines.

## Visual Identity

### Color Palette

**Primary Colors**

| Color | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| R.T.A. Orange | `#ef6825` | Primary brand color. CTAs, accents, card borders, highlights, logo. The energy of the brand. |
| R.T.A. Dark Orange | `#d55212` | Hover states, gradients, secondary buttons, icon backgrounds. Depth and warmth. |
| R.T.A. Black | `#000000` | Headlines, navigation background, primary text. The authority of the brand. |

**Neutral Colors**

| Color | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Charcoal | `#343a40` | Body text, secondary headings, supporting copy |
| Medium Gray | `#495057` | Captions, metadata, secondary information |
| Light Gray | `#777777` | Muted text, placeholders, disabled states |
| Off-White | `#f9f9f9` | Section backgrounds, alternating rows, subtle separation |
| White | `#ffffff` | Page backgrounds, text on dark backgrounds, cards |

**Functional Colors**

| Color | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Success Green | `#16b775` | Confirmations, positive metrics, "on time/on budget" indicators in case studies |
| Alert Red | `#d33a37` | Errors, warnings — use sparingly |
| Warning Yellow | `#ffc107` | Badges, attention flags |

**Color Rules:**
- Orange is the accent, not the background. Never use orange as a full-page or full-section background.
- Black + white is the canvas. Orange draws the eye.
- The orange gradient (`#ef6825` → `#d55212`, left to right) is reserved for primary CTA buttons only.
- B2B materials: lean heavier on black + white with orange as punctuation — reads more professional.
- Consumer materials: orange can be more playful and prominent.
- Success green for positive outcomes in case studies (on-time, on-budget indicators).

### Typography

| Role | Font | Weight | Size (Desktop) |
|------|------|--------|---------------|
| H1 — Page hero | Circular Std | Bold (700) | 60px. Max 8 words. Used once per page. |
| H2 — Section heading | Circular Std | Bold (700) | 36px |
| H3 — Subsection | Circular Std | Bold (700) | 30px |
| H4 — Supporting | Circular Std | Bold (700) | 20px |
| Body text | Circular Std | Book (400) | 18px. Line-height: 1.667 |
| Small / Caption | Circular Std | Book (400) | 14px |
| Button text | Circular Std | Book/Medium (400/600) | 16px |

**Typography Rules:**
- Circular Std is the only font. No mixing with other typefaces.
- Headlines are always Bold (700), never light or italic.
- Body text is always Book (400). Never bold for full paragraphs.
- Keep headlines short. If it needs two lines, it's too long.
- Fallback: `sans-serif` (system default).
- Social media: platform defaults are fine — the voice carries the identity.
- Printed materials: embed Circular Std in PDFs.

### Imagery Style

**Photography Categories**

| Category | Direction |
|----------|-----------|
| Completed projects | Exterior architectural shots + drone/aerial. Hero images for case studies. Shoot at golden hour. |
| People | **Must add.** Project managers, team on-site, handshake at handover. The brand is built on personal service — show the people. Target: 30% of all imagery includes people. |
| Interiors | Add to show quality of finish. Important for convincing developers of end-product quality. |
| In-progress | Add selectively. Clean construction sites communicate professionalism. Developers want to see process. |
| Scale indicators | Drone shots of multi-unit projects. Side-by-side progress shots. Proves capability for large-scale work. |

**Photography Rules:**
- Always professional quality for website and sales materials. LinkedIn can use authentic on-site phone photos.
- Natural light preferred.
- Real team members only. Never use stock photos.
- Every case study needs a hero image: completed project, well-lit, best angle.
- Drone/aerial shots are a differentiator — keep investing in these.
- Ensure R.T.A. branding is visible in work-in-progress shots (branded workwear, signage, vehicles).

**Image Don'ts:**
- No stock photography. Ever.
- No dark, muddy construction site photos.
- No photos where branding isn't visible on work-in-progress shots.
- No cluttered before/after comparisons — keep it clean.

### Logo Usage

**The Logo:** Diamond shape with arrow extension, black fill, orange border, house and brick illustrations in white, "RTA" in orange, "CONSTRUCT" below.

**Variations**

| Variation | Usage |
|-----------|-------|
| Full color (orange + black on white) | Primary — website, printed materials, proposals |
| Reversed (orange + white on black) | Dark backgrounds, header navigation, event banners |
| Monochrome black | Formal documents, single-color print |
| Monochrome white | Photo overlays, dark photography backgrounds |

**Logo Rules:**
- Clear space: minimum 1x diamond height on all sides.
- Minimum size: 40px height (digital), 15mm (print).
- Position: top-left of documents, center or left in website header.
- Never stretch, rotate, recolor, or add effects to the logo.
- Never place full-color logo on busy photographic backgrounds — use monochrome white version on photos.

**Future consideration:** As B2B positioning matures, consider a simplified "RTA" wordmark (without illustrative elements) for professional B2B contexts — LinkedIn, proposals, event materials. Not an immediate change.

### Layout Principles

| Principle | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| Whitespace | Generous. Let content breathe. Whitespace signals confidence and quality. |
| Grid | Clear columns. Left-aligned text. No centered paragraphs. |
| Content-to-image ratio | 50/50 for website and case studies. Visuals prove the words. |
| Card pattern | Orange top border (`0.5rem solid #ef6825`) is a signature element. Carry to all card-based layouts. |
| CTA placement | One clear CTA per section. Don't stack competing actions. "Plan een kennismaking" is the primary B2B action. |
| Hierarchy | Every page/document: Headline → Subheadline → Visual → Body → CTA. No exceptions. |
