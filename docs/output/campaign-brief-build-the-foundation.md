## Creative Direction

### Campaign Concept
**"Eén ploeg. Elke keer."** (One team. Every time.) — In construction, the biggest variable isn't the design or the budget — it's who actually shows up to build. R.T.A. removes that variable.

### Why It Works
Developers' #1 pain point is unreliable contractors. This concept directly hits the nerve — it doesn't talk about quality in the abstract, it talks about the people who deliver it. It's inherently visual (real faces, real team, real sites), immediately differentiating (no competitor makes this claim), and campaign-able across all channels and formats.

### Tone & Mood
- **We are:** Assured, grounded, personal, precise
- **We are NOT:** Corporate, salesy, timid, vague
- **The audience should feel:** Relief. "Finally, a builder I don't have to worry about."

### Headline Options
1. **"Eén ploeg. Elke keer."** — Campaign anchor. Short, rhythmic, memorable. Immediately differentiates from the subcontractor model. Use across all assets.
2. **"U kent ons team nog voor de eerste steen."** — Personal relationship angle. Challenges the industry norm. Strong LinkedIn post opener.
3. **"Geen onderaannemers. Geen verrassingen."** — Pain point direct hit. Connects in-house team to the "no surprises" brand promise. Use on website and supporting content.
4. **"Uw projectleider staat op de werf. Niet achter een bureau."** — Accountability angle. Highlights single-contact model. Use for team spotlight content.
5. **"Dezelfde standaard. Dezelfde mensen. Elk project."** — Reliability at scale. Addresses "can they scale?" concern. Use in proposals and developer-facing materials.

### Visual Direction
- **Color:** Black + white dominant canvas. R.T.A. orange (`#ef6825`) used sparingly — one accent per composition. High contrast. The palette says "serious about building, not about marketing."
- **Imagery:** Real photography only. The team on-site in branded workwear. Construction in progress — steel, concrete, structure going up. Drone shots for scale. Golden-hour exteriors for completed projects. At least one face in every composition. No stock photos.
- **Typography:** Circular Std Bold for headlines — big, tight tracking, commanding. Short lines. Body in Circular Std Book — clean, readable, generous line spacing.
- **Layout:** Clean, minimal, structured. Hero image (full-width or 60/40 split) with headline overlaid. One CTA per view. Plenty of whitespace. Orange accent only on CTA button and one other element (card border, divider, stat highlight).
- **Data treatment:** Visualize key numbers prominently: "4,7/5", "0 meerkosten". Use success green (`#16b775`) for positive metrics. Numbers should be large — they're the proof points.

### References
- **Volvo's "made by people" campaigns** — Substance and emotional reassurance. Safety → reliability. "We obsess over what matters."
- **Premium architecture firm portfolio** — Clean visual precision, project-led storytelling, confident restraint.
- The intersection: not flashy, not trying too hard. Just undeniably good at what they do.
